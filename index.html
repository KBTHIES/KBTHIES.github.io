My Story

My journey into making began not in a traditional classroom, but in a workshop where I felt more at home. As a student in architecture school, I realized early on that the conventional academic path wasn’t my calling. However, I found value in the skills I was learning, especially those related to craftsmanship. More importantly, I discovered the true power of hands-on learning in an interdisciplinary makerspace, where people from all backgrounds and fields came together to explore and create. It was there that I recognized how transformative access to a creative space could be for anyone.

In my teaching experience, I’ve had the privilege of guiding individuals through their first experiences with technology, from laser cutting to sewing and coding. Yet, the phrase I heard most often from these newcomers was always the same: "I wish I had found this earlier." This moment always reminded me of the untapped potential waiting to be unlocked in every person. I made it my mission to ensure that no one would have to wait to experience the joy and empowerment that comes with making.

Through teaching, improving makerspaces, and connecting with others in the field, I’ve come to understand that creating a supportive, accessible environment for creativity is just as important as providing the right tools. Everyone should have access to the resources they need to create—and I want to be part of making that happen.

About Me

Hi there! I’m Kevin Thies — a maker, teacher, gamer, and storyteller who loves turning creative sparks into tangible creations. Whether it’s building props for LARP, designing magical girl TTRPGs, or crafting custom resin dice, I’m always chasing the joy of creating and sharing that joy with others.

When I’m not in a makerspace wielding my trusty heat gun or running a laser cutter, you’ll probably find me at a digital tabletop surrounded by dice, maps, and friends. I’m a passionate TTRPG enthusiast currently in three campaigns (yes, three!), exploring systems like Pathfinder 2e, CAIN, and Lancer. On top of that, I’m the founder and head designer of Mahou Showdown, where I release original games inspired by my favorite anime, Puella Magi Madoka Magica. Making one Magical Girl TTRPG a year has become a tradition, and if you’re curious, you can check them out on my Itch.io page on the left!

Speaking of storytelling, I also love live-action roleplaying (LARP). It’s like combining theater and improvisation with the thrill of adventure — dressing up, running through the woods, and clashing foam swords with friends. My background in film and theater acting makes it a natural fit, and I’ve even run cosplay workshops (ironically, as someone who doesn’t cosplay much).

Making has always been at the core of what I do. During college, every gift I gave was handmade — there’s something special about creating something personal for someone else. Digital fabrication tools like laser cutters are my go-to for quick iteration and high-quality results.

And because I can’t resist a fun challenge, I once ventured into streaming. Inspired by the rise of VTubers, I took to the digital stage to learn about video editing, marketing, and online creativity. Those lessons continue to shape how I present my projects and ideas.

Through it all, my goal is simple: to explore, create, and inspire others to do the same. If any of this sounds like your kind of thing, feel free to reach out or explore my portfolio!
